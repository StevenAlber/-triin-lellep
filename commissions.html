How this works

The room is the brief. Everything else is in writing.

A private commission is a single, complete cinematic vocal event — composed, performed, and produced for one room, one evening, one host. It is not a recital. It is not a concert. It is not background music for a larger programme. It is a ninety-minute artistic work, structured as a single arc, delivered acoustically in the host's interior.

Everything about the engagement is written down before any conversation takes place: the format, what is included, what is additional, the honorarium, the deposit, the timeline, the cancellation terms. Once a date is reserved, sustained discussion of artistic detail begins — directly with the artist.

We do not hold long calls before payment. We have learned that this consumes the artist's working time without producing serious commissions. If you are ready to commission, the path is short and clear. If you are not yet ready, the materials on this page are designed to answer your questions in writing.

Commission Terms

Clarity, in writing, before any commitment.

Cancellation by the host

More than 90 days before the event: 50% of paid deposits refunded.
30–90 days before: deposits non-refundable; final instalment is waived if not yet paid.
Less than 30 days before: full honorarium remains payable; production costs already committed are owed in addition.

Cancellation by the artist

In the unlikely event of artist cancellation due to illness, family emergency, or force majeure, all paid deposits are returned in full within 7 working days. We will offer to re-book within 12 months at no additional honorarium increase if the host wishes.

Documentation rights

The artist retains full copyright over the work and any film, sound, or photography produced during the event. Hosts may request private documentation rights for personal archives at additional cost. The work itself is never licensed for resale or rebroadcast.

Confidentiality

The host's identity, the room, and the guests are held in confidence by the artist and her team. Public mention of any commission is made only with explicit written consent of the host. Discretion is the default.

Production responsibility

KRYONIS Cultural Lab coordinates all production, logistics, contracts, and payments. Triin engages with the host on artistic matters only — programme, room, instruments, light, atmosphere — and only after a deposit has been confirmed.

Governing law

The commission agreement is between the host and Triin Lellep, a private cultural practitioner based in the European Union. Estonian law applies. Any dispute is resolved personally first; only if that fails are formal channels engaged.

Frequently Asked

The direct answers.

Why is no introductory call available before payment?

Triin spends her preparation time composing, not selling. We have learned that long pre-payment calls consume working time without producing serious commitments. The materials on this site, including the doctrine paper, are designed to answer all questions in writing. Once a deposit is confirmed, direct artistic dialogue with Triin begins immediately.

Can the work be performed in languages other than those listed?

Opus I is performed in English, French, Italian, and Latin — these are composed into the work. Bespoke commissions in other languages can be discussed for Souverain-format engagements once a deposit is confirmed. Translation does not happen on a recital basis.

Will Triin perform a programme of standard repertoire by request?

No. Private Écoute is an original work, not a song programme. Hosts who wish to commission a specific repertoire — operatic arias, art songs, popular ballads — are politely advised that they are seeking a different category of artist.

Is the artist available for shorter formats — 30 or 45 minutes?

The work is composed as a single ninety-minute arc. Shortening breaks the architecture. Hosts who require a brief musical presence within a larger evening should consider a different artist; the cinematic vocal structure does not adapt to interval slots.

Can two private écoutes be commissioned on the same trip?

Yes — within the same region, two engagements separated by at least 48 hours can be arranged. The second is offered at preferred terms (reduced honorarium, shared logistics). This is discussed at proposal stage.

What happens if my room turns out to be acoustically unsuitable?

A pre-event walkthrough — remote or on-site — is included in every commission. If acoustic concerns arise, alternative configurations are proposed: instrument substitution, room re-arrangement, or in rare cases, a venue change. The work always sounds as composed; that is the artistic standard.

Can the engagement include a meet-and-greet or signing?

The artist remains present after the écoute for a brief reception of up to forty-five minutes. Formal signing sessions, panel discussions, or photo calls are not part of the standard commission and are not offered. The work itself is the encounter.

What currencies and payment methods are accepted?

EUR, USD, GBP, CHF, and major Asian currencies. Payment by SEPA / SWIFT bank transfer is preferred for amounts above €12,500. Stripe (card) and cryptocurrency (BTC, ETH, USDC) are accepted for deposits and smaller honoraria.
