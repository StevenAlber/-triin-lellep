The Invitation

A category does not begin alone.

Every art form that becomes a category — from chamber music in private salons to the modern intimate retreat — was first held in being by a small circle of people who believed in it before the world did. Triin Lellep's work is at this moment.

She is a composer-soprano — she writes the music she sings. She is also an author, a stage artist, and the architect of Private Écoute itself: a ninety-minute cinematic vocal work, performed acoustically in private interiors, structured as a single arc that builds from near silence to a final illuminated fullness. It is not a recital. It is not a concert. It is a complete cinematic composition for voice, instrument, light and room.

The first private écoute will take place in Asia in June 2026. The room, the host, the guests, the camera, the documentation, the photography — these together establish what the work is, both as a vocal practice and as a category that did not exist before.

We are inviting thirty-one patrons to make this first chapter possible. The number is intentional. The relationship is personal. Each patron receives something real in return — not gratitude, but objects, films, and presence that no public market will sell.

On Integrity

A few things you should know.

This is not crowdfunding

Private Écoute is positioned in the international luxury cultural market. The Écoute Circle is not a public donation campaign. It is a closed circle whose members will be remembered in the artist's career as those who held the beginning. There is no progress bar, no public pledge counter, no comments section.

If we cannot fill the circle

If by 31 May 2026 the full €70,500 is not committed, the work will still launch — at a smaller scale, with what is gathered. Every patron will still receive their full benefits. Nothing is held hostage to a fundraising target.

Documentation and accounting

Every patron receives, after the first Asian écoute, a private financial summary of how the funds were used. This is not investor reporting in the venture-capital sense — it is courtesy between the artist and the people who made the work possible.

Why thirty-one

The number is small enough that every patron is known to the artist personally, and large enough that the chapter is held by a circle, not by a single benefactor. This is the architecture, not a marketing decision.
