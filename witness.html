"

Some chapters need witnesses — those who choose, before the work is widely known, to stand close to it. Not to own it. Not to direct it. Simply to be there, named, when it begins.

If this is the form your support wants to take, this page is for you. There is no further patronage expected after this single act. One door, opened fully, once. The rest is the work itself, and what it becomes.

— Triin Lellep
What you receive

What arrives after your contribution.

i.

Everything from the previous tiers

The five songs in high resolution. The handwritten letter from Triin. The three-minute cinematic film and the twelve-minute documentary. The signed printed photographic plate.

ii.

A private listening session with Triin

A live, real conversation by video or in person — forty-five minutes, scheduled at a time you choose, in a window of two months from your contribution. You may simply listen; you may ask anything; you may also be silent. The session is not recorded.

iii.

Your name in the founding patron document

If you wish — and only if you wish — your full name, or initials, or a chosen mark, will be inscribed on the page of founding patrons in the cultural document that accompanies the inaugural écoute. Held privately by the studio; circulated only among the founding circle and selected cultural archives.

iv.

First-room option for any future écoute

Should you ever wish to host a private écoute in your own room — for yourself, for a small gathering, for an institution — your inquiry comes first, before any new commission. This is not a guarantee of availability; it is a guarantee of being heard first.

Why this form

Four hundred and fifty euros is a meaningful contribution. It is not a fee, and it is not a price. It is the sum chosen by those who want to stand near the beginning — to be quietly part of the first chapter's emergence, without needing to commission it.

At this tier, you become known to the studio as a person, not as a transaction. The private listening session is real, and Triin's time for it is held with care. The session is not a performance; it is closer to a meeting between two people who already share something.

What you give does not pay Triin. It funds the first chapter — and it makes you, quietly, part of how it began.
